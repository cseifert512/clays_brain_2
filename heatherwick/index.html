Case study 1

Archipedia: making precedent searchable as design evidence

Problem

Architectural knowledge is trapped in folders, PDFs, image boards, and individual memory.

System

Visual embeddings, metadata, FAISS search, and a React/FastAPI interface.

User value

Designers can search by visual, spatial, and material similarity rather than relying only on keywords.

Heatherwick value

A studio archive becomes an active design resource, not dead storage.

Case study 2

From whole-image similarity to part-level design reasoning

A lot of architectural ML stops at "this image looks like that image." My research asks whether we can retrieve or compare at the level of parts, operations, assemblies, and latent geometric logic.

Fragment input

Architectural imagery becomes a field of meaningful local cues.

Patch tokens

ViT-style representations make local spatial signals comparable.

Embedding neighborhood

Clusters can expose similar operations, assemblies, or formal tendencies.

Retrieved logic

Search can move toward extrusion, revolution, truncation, boolean logic, and part relationships.

Case study 3

Making technical constraints visible during design

Problem

Designers often get structural or geometric feedback too late.

Prototype

Browser-based real-time structural feedback with GPU acceleration as a technical exploration.

Broader point

Computation should not live only in specialist tools; it can become part of the design interaction loop.

Product and adoption

I think about tools as workflows, not just models
